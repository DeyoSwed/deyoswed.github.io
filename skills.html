Skills

Hard vs soft skills

Although I've acquired most of my technical skills in recent years through a combination of schooling and self-study, I've also developed strong analytical and communication skills through various other experiences. Throughout my career, I've found that my interest in technology has been a significant strength, particularly in my ability to identify areas for improvement and find creative solutions to problems. I'm the type of person who never accepts something at face value and is always looking for ways to make things better. To further reinforce this trait, I'm not afraid to provide detailed feedback to companies when I feel their products or services could be improved. For example, I once sent a three-page email to Google Smart Home detailing how their service could be more efficient for my sixty smart devices. Another time, I provided thorough customer feedback to a printer leasing service regarding the automation process and was rewarded with a Bose speaker for my efforts. I can be a bit OCD when it comes to efficiency and avoiding wasted resources, but I believe that this mindset has led to a lot of successful problem-solving throughout my career.

Data science

I was first introduced to the field of data science less than two years ago when I was looking for ways to maximize the utilization of my Excel spreadsheets. Prior to the pandemic, I had considered learning Python out of curiosity, but never felt a pressing need to do so in my challenging and exciting career. When the pandemic hit and travel restrictions made it difficult to achieve our ambitious milestones for Private Labels at Ahlsell Norge AS, I turned to statistical analysis tools and techniques such as correlation and causality to help drive improvements. I quickly gained a reputation for my ability to provide valuable improvement advice to our 700 sales employees, but soon realized that both my communication skills and Excel were inadequate for the high-level changes that I wanted to make. It was then that I realized that what I was doing was data science. I could either continue to be frustrated by my limitations or take action to improve my skills. I ultimately chose Noroff as my educational institution after an extensive search of available programs. I found that a bachelor's or equivalent in Applied Data Science is a rare program, and after researching the field further, I was impressed with the depth and breadth of Noroff's curriculum.
